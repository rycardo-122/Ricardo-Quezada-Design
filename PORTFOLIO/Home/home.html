For DES 050 (intro to 3D design) My group of 2 other classmates and myself ideated, prototyped, and built a life size version of our render. The name of our project ended up being titled the U-Chair. We worked on Rhino to come up with the 3D renders of our chair. With this data we were able to collect the correct measurements through the exploded iso and through illustrtor as a 2D file which we then sent out to the CNC cutter.

With the help of the CNC cutter at our design building we were able to seamlessly cut out all the parts for our chair. WHen designing the chair we wanted to be able to create a product that would be interactive with the user. We built an elongated version of the chair, we then implemented hinges in the middle which help with splitting up the chair. These hinhes allow one side of the chair to fold open into another chair allowing two users to interact with the U-chair at once.

During the summer of 2020 I enrolled into a UX/UI bootcamp at UC Irvine where I was able to learn about user research and interface design. During the last few weeks of this course my team and I which consited of 4 people worked on complete website redesign. We noticed that the website was lacking several things that are pivotal for a good user experience. The main thing that we wanted to do to fix the site was to make it accessible for all tyoes of users who might have dissabilities.

We also focused on organizing the site to make more sense when navigating through the nav bar. The user flow of this site was fixed after we conducted a few user tests on the old site and then on our demos which were lofi iterations of the original site.
